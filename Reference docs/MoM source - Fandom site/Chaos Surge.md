[SpellIcon ChaosSurge](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/e/e9/SpellIcon_ChaosSurge.png/revision/latest?cb=20180111231706)

**Chaos Surge** is a [Very Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare_Spell "Very Rare Spell") [Global Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Global_Enchantment "Global Enchantment") that belongs to the [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm") of [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Magic](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**. For [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **1,000**, it may be cast on the overland map to bestow several bonuses to *all* creatures associated with the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** Realm.

Each such creature, regardless of ownership or location, receives a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **+2** to its [Melee Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength#Melee_Strength "Attack Strength") (if it has a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") at all), a **+2** to its [Ranged Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength#Ranged_Attack_Strength "Attack Strength") (if it has a [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") of any kind), and a **+2** bonus to any direct damage short-range attack it possesses ([Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Fire Breath**, [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attacks**, or [Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze") [Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze")). These bonuses linger as long as **Chaos Surge** is in effect, and are cumulative with any other bonuses.

**Chaos Surge** requires a very high [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **40** per turn to remain in operation.

## Effects

**Chaos Surge** increases the magical energy flowing from the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm")
into all creatures associated with it, thus making them all
significantly more powerful in battle. While in effect, the spell
bestows three separate bonuses - though each unit will only receive
bonuses to [Attack Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Type "Attack Type") it can already make use of.

*Each and every* creature that belongs to the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** Realm is affected, regardless of its location in the game world or its allegiance. Even neutral [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** units guarding [Encounter Zones](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Encounter_Zone "Encounter Zone") will be affected. This also includes [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") or [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") that have been [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channeled](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")**, as they are now considered to belong to the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** Realm as well.

### Melee Attack Bonus

While **Chaos Surge** is in effect, each affected creature that already has a [Melee Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength#Melee_Strength "Attack Strength") of at least [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **1** will receive a bonus of [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **+2**. This increases the maximum potential [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** that the unit can deliver when making a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") or [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack") (and vicariously its average [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** output in these cases). This, in turn, also makes it easier for them to [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** better-armored enemy targets.

Note that in [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure Units**, *each figure* will utilize this bonus, so the total Melee Strength of a fully healthy [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** unit can be significantly increased as a result. For example, a [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **4-figure** unit of [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Hell Hounds](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hell_Hounds "Hell Hounds")** receives [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **+2** to *each Hound's attack*, increasing the potential [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** output of the unit by [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **+8** in total! However, because any targets attacked by this unit will still roll [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block** separately for each attacking [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure**, the actual [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** increase will typically be much lower than what a +8 bonus to a single attack would suggest.

### Ranged Attack Bonus

Similarly, as long as **Chaos Surge** is in play, affected creatures that have a [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile Attack** will receive a bonus of [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **+2**. The same applies to creatures using [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **Ranged Boulder Attacks** and [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attacks**, granting [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **+2** and [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **+2** respectively. Just like the [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee** bonus, this increases the potential (and average) [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** of each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** in the unit, and applies to each and every one of them simultaneously.

For example, an [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **8-figure** unit of [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos-Channeled](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")** [Slingers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Slingers "Slingers") receives [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **+2** to *each Slinger's attack*, increasing the potential [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** output of the entire unit by a total of [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **+16**! The same as with Melee though, each [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **figure**'s attack will still be defended against independently. Eight separate [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **+2**s, while formidable, are still not quite the same as a single [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **+16**.

It may be worth noting that **Chaos Surge** is one of only a handful effects that can increase a non-[Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") unit's [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical** [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength").

### Short Range Bonus

Finally, **Chaos Surge**
enhances short-range attacks by the same amount if the affected unit
possesses any of these. The attacks this can typically apply to are [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Fire Breath**, [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attacks**, and the [Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze") [Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze") of the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Spawn](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Spawn "Chaos Spawn")**. The only exclusion is the [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Chaos Breath**, one of the random enhancements granted by [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")**. This attack will not be improved by **Chaos Surge**. While the spell would technically also affect all other short-range [Attack Types](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Type "Attack Type"), they will never be available on [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** units in unmodded games.

The [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** implications of this bonus are identical to those mentioned above for [Melee-](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") and regular [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack"). **Chaos Surge** is also one of only very few effects that can universally improve short-range attacks (although [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attacks** can be improved by other means).

## Usage

**Chaos Surge** may be cast only on the overland map, for the substantial [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **1,000**. Immediately, each unit associated with the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** begins enjoying the above bonuses, and will continue to do so for as long as the spell remains in effect.

**Chaos Surge** applies to *all* [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**
creatures, including the caster's own units, their rivals' units, and
any neutral creatures encountered anywhere in the game world. New [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** units created while the spell is already in operation will also enjoy its effects, as will any units modified through [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")**.

To maintain this spell, an [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **40** must be paid at the start of each turn. If there is insufficient [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** in the caster's [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** Pool to pay for **Chaos Surge**, it will automatically dissipate.

After casting the spell, its name will appear in the "Overland Enchantments" list on the *Magic* screen. The color in which a name is printed indicates which [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") controls that particular spell. In the case of the human player, they may cancel **Chaos Surge**
manually from here, at any time, by clicking on its name. The primary
reason to do this would be in order to remove the spell's [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost"), thus conserving [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** for other spells.

Note that two or more **Chaos Surge** spells cast by different Wizards *are not cumulative* with each other. As long as at least one of these spells remains in operation, all valid creatures will receive exactly **+2** to the appropriate attributes. The only reason to cast another **Chaos Surge**
if one is already in play is to ensure that the spell remains in
operation if the other Wizard(s) decide to cancel theirs (or simply run
out of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** to keep them up).

At least, this is the case in game versions where a related bug ([see below](#Known_Bugs)) is no longer an issue.

## Acquisition

As a [Very Rare Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare_Spell "Very Rare Spell"), **Chaos Surge** may never be acquired at the start of the campaign, regardless of how many Spellbooks the Wizard possesses. It must either be [Researched](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research "Research") during the game, or obtained through other means. **Chaos Surge** may become available for research to any [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") who possesses at least [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**3** [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook"). However, this availability is not guaranteed unless the Wizard obtains, or starts with, at least [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**10** books.

Wizards with [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**3** to [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**9** Spellbooks have a random chance of being able to research **Chaos Surge**, which increases with the amount of [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**
Spellbooks possessed or acquired during the game. The spell may
otherwise be obtained by trading; found during the conquest of the [Fortress](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fortress "Fortress") of a rival Wizard who already knows it; or won as a reward from an [Encounter Zone](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Encounter_Zone "Encounter Zone"). However, in all of these cases the minimum requirement of [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**3** Spellbooks still applies.

**Chaos Surge** has a [Research Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Cost "Research Cost") of [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **3,500**, although its research may be sped up by the [Chaos Mastery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Mastery "Chaos Mastery") and/or [Sage Master](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sage_Master "Sage Master") [Retorts](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort"), or a bookshelf containing [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**8** or more.

## Strategy

**Chaos Surge** can confer an unbelievably powerful advantage to any Wizard relying primarily on creatures from the [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**, including Wizards who make a habit of casting [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")** on their [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") (thereby turning them into [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** creatures). In conjunction with [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Doom Mastery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Mastery "Doom Mastery")**, it is possible to create very powerful armies with great ease.

On the downside, rival Wizards using [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** will, of course, experience the same benefit, and could thus capitalize on the effects of **Chaos Surge** to advance their own agendas. It may or may not be a good idea to cast this spell when involved in a war with such a Wizard.

Finally, while the spell is in effect, it will be significantly harder for any wizard to conquer [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Node "Chaos Node")**. This may be either beneficial (if an enemy Wizard is attempting to take such [Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Node "Node")) or detrimental (if the caster is hoping to take them).

As mentioned above, **Chaos Surge** is one of very few ways to improve certain types of attacks. This pertains mainly to [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attacks**, [Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Fire Breath**, and [Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze") [Doom Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Gaze "Doom Gaze"). Naturally, [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Node "Chaos Node")** Auras will also benefit these abilities for [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**-related [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit"), but for Normal Units, the only other options are [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") **Experience** or turning the units into [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead").

[Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Experience Levels** are cumulative with enhancements from either [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** or [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**, provided that they are earned *before* the unit is turned into a creature of either of these [Realms](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"). However, the two Realms generally *exclude* one another. With that in mind, **Chaos Surge** is typically a better large scale investment, although the combination of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Darkness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Darkness "Darkness")** and [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Black Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Channels "Black Channels")** provides a higher overall boost.

However, [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Black Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Channels "Black Channels")** must be individually preserved on each unit for this, while [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Doom Mastery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Mastery "Doom Mastery")** provides a convenient way of affecting all newly created units with [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")**.
In addition, the abilities gained through this can be quite potent, and
can not be dispelled by rivals in combat either. Furthermore, this
combination has excellent synergy with [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Warp Reality](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warp_Reality "Warp Reality")** to increase the staying power of the Wizard's forces, as it will only penalize the [To Hit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/To_Hit "To Hit") **To Hit** values of the opponents (unless, of course, they are also creatures of [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**).

## Known Bugs

In version v1.31 (and also in [Insecticide](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Insecticide "Insecticide")), the **Chaos Surge** spell will only exert *any*
effect if it is cast by the human player. While the code does contain
the basics of a loop designed to check the spell's existence for all
Wizards, unfortunately when it does so it always checks the same memory
address that belongs to the human player. This means not only that the
AI Wizards' **Chaos Surges**
can be safely ignored (as they have no effect), if the human player
desires the benefits of this spell, they can only gain them by casting **Chaos Surge** themselves.

This bug is fixed in the [Unofficial Patch 1.50](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unofficial_Patch_1.50 "Unofficial Patch 1.50") (W141FIX.TXT).
