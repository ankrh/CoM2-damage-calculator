The [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death Realm** is one of the six magical [Realms](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm") in *Master of Magic*. It combines the armies of the [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") with a slew of spells that weaken enemy units and entire economies, making it harder for the enemy to fight at all.

The **Death Realm** specializes in [Cursing](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Type "Spell Type") enemy assets, and has far more spells of this type than any other realm. Its creatures are all [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead"),
an attribute that makes them very powerful and resilient, but incapable
of healing. They tend to be cheap and plentiful however - overwhelming
the enemy with sheer numbers. The **Death Realm** relies on weakening
the enemy, rather than strengthening itself, and often sacrifices its
own strength to acquire greater gains in the long run. It is also
capable of stealing the enemy's strength to bolster itself. The **Death Realm** is strongly opposed to the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** Realm, and may be either particularly effective against it or particularly vulnerable to it, depending on the circumstances.

The **Death Realm** is associated with the color black (or
purple), and the concepts of darkness, disease, corruption, terror, and
unholy sacrifice. It is often marked with the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Skull** symbol, both in-game and on this Wiki. The familiar associated with the **Death Realm** is the cat, seen at the top of this page.

## Overview

The [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death Realm**
concentrates on combative spells to raise undead armies, curse and
wither enemy forces, and gnaw away at rival empires. This color is so
strongly antithetical to life magic that wizards are barred from owning [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook") of both types.

Death features Combat Instants
with lingering effects that are either strong on their own or have
synergy with other forms of attack, but using them repeatedly is
extremely costly. The same goes for its line of big-ticket [Town Curses](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town_Curse "Town Curse").
On harder difficulty settings, players find that these spells seem to
get lost amid the AI's immense statistical advantages, though on the
same token, AI-controlled black wizards can cause misery to the human
player with them and are high-priority foes.

This realm shows its true quality for us through its [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit"), which are exceedingly cost-effective either by way of being cheap themselves or by turning their victims into [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") under the wizard's control. Raised *en masse*, undead [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") are a powerful economic and military asset.

### Description

*Master of Magic's* in-game documentation alludes to the
existence of the Death Realm. This would be the underworld, a dark
plane nestled deep below the realities of Arcanus and Myrror, populated
by demons and lost souls. It is also certain from in-game evidence that
a pantheon of evil gods exist here, who traffic in bargains and
sacrifice.

While this plane cannot be entered and explored, it is the source
of all black magic channeled by spellcasters, and home to all Fantastic
creatures of Death. A spellcaster proficient in Death magic may bind
these creatures to his will. Revenants can also be assembled by
necromancy, a branch of the realm concerned with putting mortal husks
back into active service. When destroyed, these beings plunge back into
the Death Realm or into oblivion.

The realm of the dead actively defies mortal understanding and
prevents certain mysteries of the cosmos from being uncovered. Its
magic governs the binding and purging of souls in containers, and the
evocation of darkness, nightmares, and life-draining energy. Despite
its key role in the cosmology of the game, hands-on study of Death Magic
likely involves a great deal of cruel experimentation and is only
practiced beyond the fringes of civil society, by wizards and cabalists.

## Realm Details

Death has 10 spells per degree of [Spell Rarity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Rarity "Spell Rarity"), totaling 40 spells with a cumulative research cost of [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **59,950**. Each member holds a discrete cost increment along the spectrum between [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **20** ([Skeletons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Skeletons "Skeletons")) and [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **6,000** ([Demon Lord](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Demon_Lord "Demon Lord")).

While the majority of its spells are for direct use against
enemies, Death magic also has a strong suite of summons and a branch of
unique effects concerned with [Undeath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead").

### Rival Wizards

:   *Main article: [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard")*

When the world is generated, computer-controlled wizards each make a lottery-ball style roll for [Personality](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Personality "Personality") and [Objective](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Objective "Objective").
These traits modify the wizard's behavior, giving them some
hard-to-resist compulsions and adding flavor to the match. If the
wizard's shelf is stocked primarily with [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death Spellbooks**,
this realm will convey influence on the rolls. In cases where
spellbook ranks are tied, and the game needs to decide which realm
conveys influence, precedence descends in this order:

:   [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature") [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery") [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos") [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life") [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")

Therefore, a default split-profile wizard like [Sharee](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sharee "Sharee") ([Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**5** [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**5**)
will take a personality and objective corresponding to probabilities
dictated by her chaos ranks, not her death ones, pending even further
modification by [Conjurer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conjurer "Conjurer").

Dominant Death ranks drive the wizard towards a Personality of
either "Ruthless" or "Maniacal," and an Objective of either "Militarist"
or "Expansionist" (but will not guarantee these traits). The table
below shows the chances each character trait has of appearing on a Death
wizard, before accounting for the influence of [Retorts](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort").

| [Icon Book Death](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/a/ae/Icon_Book_Death.png/revision/latest?cb=20120102024823) | Personality | | Objective | |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Maniacal | 30% | Expansionist | 40% |
| Ruthless | 30% | Militarist | 40% |
| Aggressive | 20% | Theurgist | 10% |
| Chaotic | 10% | Perfectionist | 10% |
| Lawful | 10% |
| Peaceful | -- |

Ruthless
:   On the diplomatic scale of 100 to -100, a "Ruthless" wizard has a -30 *Core Reaction*
    to all of the other wizards, including the human player, and the chance
    this wizard will elect to perform hostile actions (overland spells and
    armed assaults) is increased by 30 percentage points. As all diplomatic
    standings gravitate towards the core reaction at a speed of 1 point per
    turn, this wizard *will* declare war unless steadily appeased.

Maniacal
:   All diplomatic gains are halved, and losses are doubled. The "Maniacal" wizard also has a -50 *Core Reaction*
    to other wizards, and the chance this wizard will elect to perform
    hostile actions against them is increased by 50 percentage points. More
    than simply hating their rivals, this type of wizard is dedicated to
    attacking everything in sight, constantly, and cultivating a friendship
    with them will prove both expensive and temporary.

Expansionist
:   An "Expansionist" wizard has double the chance of building [Settlers](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Settlers "Settlers"), and will attempt to sprawl across every available piece of land.

Militarist
:   A "Militarist" has double the chance of training military units and
    casting summoning spells. Due to their reduced likelihood of
    constructing new buildings and making alternative spell choices, these
    units are likely to be primitive and unbuffed, but there will be a ton
    of them.

If left unchecked, Black wizards will gather up large armies of death creatures, and hit rivals with crippling [Town Curses](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town_Curse "Town Curse"), [Warp](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warp_Node "Warp Node") their magic nodes, and eventually inflict enormous, permanent damage with [Cruel Unminding](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cruel_Unminding "Cruel Unminding").
This last spell should absolutely not fall into your enemy's
repertoire and could be a deadline by which you would want to banish a
death wizard from the planes.

#### Black Diplomacy Modifiers

Rival wizards make the following initial adjustments to their *Core Reaction* to a Death wizard (human or otherwise):

|  |

### Fantastic Units

The [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death Realm** has ten native [Fantastic Creature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit")
types, and offers as many unique ways of spawning Death units. Seven of
the creatures are bona-fide members of the Death spell repertoire: [Skeletons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Skeletons "Skeletons"), [Ghouls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ghouls "Ghouls"), the [Night Stalker](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Night_Stalker "Night Stalker"), [Shadow Demons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Shadow_Demons "Shadow Demons"), [Wraiths](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraiths "Wraiths"), [Death Knights](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Knights "Death Knights"), and the [Demon Lord](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Demon_Lord "Demon Lord").

**Ghouls**, **Wraiths**, and **Death Knights** are highly-effective vanguard units in their time frames, able to create [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") (discussed below). Being invisible, **Night Stalkers** can scout most lairs and pick targets for their [Death Gaze](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Gaze "Death Gaze").

**Zombies** can be assembled under a wizard's control only under the global spell [Zombie Mastery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Zombie_Mastery "Zombie Mastery"). These are low-tier units, like **Skeletons**.
Both types of creatures can be massed for suicide raids or serve as
cannon-fodder and decoys when their respective spells are available.

**Werewolves** and **Shadow Demons** have the ability to regenerate and share the same general time frame. **Werewolves** are created by transforming a normal unit with a "Permanent Unit Enchantment," [Lycanthropy](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lycanthropy "Lycanthropy"). **Shadow Demons** are more expensive and versatile units.

**Demons** are called only in combat through the [Summon Demons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Summon_Demons "Summon Demons") ability possessed by **Demon Lords**; they therefore act as part of the overall combat strength of this high-grade unit.

#### Undead Units

Finally, Death magic offers means for normal and fantastic units to rejoin the game as [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") creatures under the wizard's control. Most methods involve slaying the unit with [Life-Stealing/Create Undead Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Stealing_Damage "Life Stealing Damage"), a form of damage tracked separately from damage the unit has received through other sources.

When a battle is concluded, the game runs through each and every
dead unit on the battlefield, checking what type of damage killed that
unit. If the majority of the damage that killed the unit is found to be
Life Stealing Damage, and the life-stealer's side won the battle, the
unit will rise again as an Undead unit, and will switch allegiance. It
is now on the side of the life-stealer.

For example, imagine a battle where [Wraiths](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wraiths "Wraiths") assault an enemy [High Men Cavalry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/High_Men_Cavalry "High Men Cavalry")
unit and completely destroy it. If the majority of the damage inflicted
on the Cavalry was Life Stealing Damage from the Wraiths' attack, and
the Wraiths' side went on to win this battle, then the High Men Cavalry
returns as an Undead unit, serving the Wraiths' master.

For this to happen, the majority of the damage must have been
Life Stealing Damage. The total number of units that may be reanimated
in any one battle is limited by the army stack size: 9 minus the number
of victorious units *surviving to the end of combat*, including any members of that side [Recalled](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Word_of_Recall "Word of Recall") from the battle.

[Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") are spawned following successful usage of these Death Realm effects:

* [Life Drain](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Drain "Life Drain") (spell)
* [Black Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Channels "Black Channels") (spell)
* [Animate Dead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Animate_Dead "Animate Dead") (spell)
* [Create Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Create_Undead "Create Undead") (ability)
* [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal") (special attack)
* [Vampiric](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Vampiric "Vampiric") (item power)

[Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") units take on the following new characteristics:

[Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") Fantastic Unit
:   This unit is considered a [Fantastic Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") of the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death Realm**
    for most purposes, even if it was already a fantastic unit of a
    different magic realm. The pictured "Undead" unit enchantment icon
    appears in its query window along with its other listed abilities.
    Unlike normal sustained [Unit Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment"),
    it has no upkeep cost and can never be dispelled. Units bearing this
    ability are exceptionally vulnerable to "resist or suffer" [Special Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Damage "Special Damage") from Life sources, like [Dispel Evil](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Dispel_Evil "Dispel Evil").

Altered Upkeep
:   Any [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") turned [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") will have its [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") reduced to [Food](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Food "Food")**0** and [Gold](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gold "Gold") **0**. Any [Fantastic Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") will have its [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") multiplied by [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **1½**.

Inability to Gain Experience
:   [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") units do not gain experience with time, nor with winning battles, though the [Armsmaster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Armsmaster "Armsmaster")
    ability still affects them (this is probably a bug). They retain stats
    gained by experience thus far, as well as their magic weapons - [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack")[Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack")[Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") - if they had these.

Unhealing
:   The unit does not heal naturally, and cannot be healed by any spell or abilities other than [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal") or [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration"). Thus [Trolls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Trolls "Trolls") are the ideal [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") servants.

### Unit Abilities

There are several traits common to all creatures of the Death Realm, including the [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead").

#### Unhealing

* (As above) Death units do not heal naturally every overland turn. They also cannot be healed using [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** magic spells, meaning that a Death wizard cannot support them in battle with the [Priests](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Priests "Priests")' [Healing Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Healing_Spell "Healing Spell"), for instance. They can only regain health through [Life Steal](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Steal "Life Steal") or [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration").

#### [Poison Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Immunity "Poison Immunity") [Poison Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Immunity "Poison Immunity")

* This unit cannot be hurt by [Poison Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Poison_Damage "Poison Damage"). It will automatically pass any [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** roll to avert such damage.

#### [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity") [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity")

* [Illusion Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion_Damage "Illusion Damage") inflicted on this unit does not bypass its armor. The unit gets to make its [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** rolls as normal.
* During combat, this unit will spot all enemy [Invisible](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility")
  creatures regardless of how close they are. Those creatures become
  semi-visible on the screen, and may be targeted freely like any other
  unit.
* This unit is completely immune to many [Unit Curses](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Curse "Unit Curse") from the [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")** realm. It either may not be targeted by such curses at all, or is simply not affected by them.

#### [Cold Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Immunity "Cold Immunity") [Cold Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Immunity "Cold Immunity")

* This unit cannot be affected by any spell or ability that delivers [Cold Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cold_Damage "Cold Damage"). It will simply not receive any [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** from such sources.

#### [Death Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Immunity "Death Immunity") [Death Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Immunity "Death Immunity")

* This unit cannot be affected by any spell or ability that causes death or fear, including most of the combat spells from the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**Death Realm**.

### Related Wizard Skills

#### [Infernal Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Infernal_Power "Infernal Power")

**Infernal Power** requires [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**4** ranks in Death magic and costs 2 picks. It increases the power and the pacifying effects obtained from [Shrines](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Shrine "Shrine"), [Temples](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Temple "Temple"), [Parthenons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Parthenon "Parthenon"), and [Cathedrals](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cathedral "Cathedral") by 50%. Combining the retort with [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Dark Rituals](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Dark_Rituals "Dark Rituals")** produces an extraordinary amount of [Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") **Power**.

**Divine Power** may be found by Death wizards and yields exactly the same effect as **Infernal Power**. The two retorts together do not exhibit cumulative effects.

#### [Conjurer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Conjurer "Conjurer")

**Conjurer** has no prerequisites and costs 1 pick. It reduces the casting and upkeep for all [Summoning Spells](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Summoning_Spell "Summoning Spell") by 25%, and boosts research on these spells by 25%. It lowers overhead without the need to take so many ranks in this realm.

#### [Channeler](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Channeler "Channeler")

**Channeler** has no prerequisites and costs 2 picks. It removes
the distance modifier in combat spellcasting and reduces all spell
maintenance by half. This retort loses momentum if mana becomes
plentiful but is very well-suited to the Death Realm's emphasis on
fantastic armies and combat spells.
