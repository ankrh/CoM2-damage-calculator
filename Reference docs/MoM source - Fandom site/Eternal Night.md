**Eternal Night** is a [Very Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare_Spell "Very Rare Spell") [Global Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Global_Enchantment "Global Enchantment") that belongs to the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death Magic](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"). It may be cast on the overland map for the base [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **1,000**. While **Eternal Night** is in play, *every* battle that does not take place in a [Town](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town "Town") protected by [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Heavenly Light](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Heavenly_Light "Heavenly Light")** comes under an effect virtually identical to that of the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Darkness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Darkness "Darkness")** spell. As a result, all [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** creatures gain a large number of small-but-significant increases to their combat attributes. At the same time, all [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** creatures are inflicted with a similar set of *penalties*.

**Eternal Night** has its own [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **15**, to be paid every turn to keep it in play. However the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Darkness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Darkness "Darkness")** effect is put into play in each battle at no additional cost, preserving [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** that would otherwise be spent on casting it. Furthermore, while **Eternal Night** can be [Disjuncted](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Disjunction "Disjunction"), nothing can be done to remove the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Darkness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Darkness "Darkness")** effect once a battle is already underway.

## Effects

**Eternal Night** covers the entire world in dark energy, automatically shrouding every battle in [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Darkness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Darkness "Darkness")**. This gives great benefits to creatures of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**, who thrive in the dark; while simultaneously weakening creatures of [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**.

### Darkness

:   *Main article: [Darkness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Darkness "Darkness")*

While **Eternal Night** is in effect, each and every battle anywhere in the world is considered as being under the effect of the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Darkness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Darkness "Darkness")** spell, unless it is taking place in a [Town](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town "Town") protected by [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Heavenly Light](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Heavenly_Light "Heavenly Light")**.

[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Darkness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Darkness "Darkness")** gives the following bonuses to each and every unit associated with the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"), including any [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") units:

* [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **+1** bonus to [Melee Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength#Melee_Strength "Attack Strength") (provided that the unit has a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack")).
* [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **+1** (or [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **+1** or [Ranged Boulder Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Boulder_Attack "Ranged Boulder Attack") **+1**, as appropriate) bonus to [Ranged Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") Strength (if the unit possesses a Ranged Attack at all).
* **+1** bonus to any short-range [Special Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Special_Attack "Special Attack") the unit may have ([Fire Breath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Breath "Fire Breath") **Fire Breath**, [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attacks**, and any [Physical Damage](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Physical_Damage "Physical Damage") component of [Gaze Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack"), hidden or otherwise).
* [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+1** bonus to the unit's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") score.
* [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **+1** bonus to the unit's [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") score.

Simultaneously, [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Darkness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Darkness "Darkness")** also gives the following penalties to each and every [Fantastic Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") that belongs to the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** Realm:

* [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **-1**
  penalty to Melee Strength (while this effect would also decrease other
  Attack Strengths, the affected units will never have any in the official
  game);
* [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **-1** penalty to Defense;
* [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **-1** penalty to Resistance.

All of these adjustments apply on a per-[Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figure** basis, making them significantly stronger when they affect [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** units. While nearly all [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** creatures are unfortunately [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Single-Figure_Units "Figure") **Single Figures**, making the negative effect even less potent than it otherwise could be; both natural [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** units and [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") often have multiple [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure "Figure") **Figures**, and can make excellent use of the positive enhancements.

Every unit on the battlefield associated with the Realms of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** or [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** is affected by **Eternal Night**, regardless of their allegiance. However, a [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[True Light](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/True_Light "True Light")** spell cast by the opponent can cancel out all of these, resulting in a net zero benefit to either party.

### Automatic Area Effect

**Eternal Night** does not actually cast [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Darkness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Darkness "Darkness")** each time the battle begins. Instead, it has an area effect that does not require any casting (nor any expenditure of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana**), and *cannot be dispelled* during the battle. Subsequently, [Darkness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Darkness "Darkness") **Darkness** will not appear in the [Combat Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Enchantment "Combat Enchantment") panel of the battle screen, and Eternal Night **Eternal Night** is instead displayed on the list of external effects, which can be viewed by clicking on the "Info" button in combat.

Note that it is nonetheless impossible to cast [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Darkness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Darkness "Darkness")** during a battle to gain cumulative bonuses with **Eternal Night**.
The game will object to this, claiming that the spell is already in
effect. This remains true even if the above benefits are cancelled out
by the opponent casting [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[True Light](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/True_Light "True Light")**.

## Usage

**Eternal Night** may only be cast on the overland map, for a high [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **1,000**. To keep the spell in effect, the casting [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") must pay an [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") of exactly [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **15** per turn. Failure to pay this cost due to lack of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** will cause **Eternal Night** to dissipate.

After casting it, the spell's name will appear on the "Overland
Enchantments" list of the Magic Screen. The color in which the name is
printed indicates which Wizard controls the enchantment. The player can
cancel their own **Eternal Night** spell from here manually at any time by clicking on its name. The primary reason to do this would be in order to remove its [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost"), thus conserving [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** for other spells.

## Acquisition

As a [Very Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare_Spell "Very Rare Spell") [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** spell, **Eternal Night** may become available to any Wizard who possesses at least [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**3** [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook"). However, its availability during the game is not guaranteed unless the Wizard starts with, or otherwise obtains, at least [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**10** Spellbooks. It may not be acquired at the start of the campaign.

Wizards with [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**3** to [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**9** Spellbooks have a random chance of being able to [Research](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research "Research") **Eternal Night** during the game. The chances of this spell appearing for research increases with the number of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** Spellbooks the Wizard possesses or gains during gameplay. With [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**10** or [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**11** books, the spell is guaranteed to show up for research at some point, unless obtained by other means.

**Eternal Night** has a [Research Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Cost "Research Cost") of [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **4,000**, although its research may be sped up by the [Sage Master](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sage_Master "Sage Master") [Retort](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort"), or a bookshelf containing [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**8** or more.

With at least [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**3** Spellbooks, **Eternal Night** may also be acquired as [Treasure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Treasure "Treasure") for defeating [Encounter Zones](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Encounter_Zone "Encounter Zone"), or found when conquering the [Fortress](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fortress "Fortress") of a rival [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") who has already researched this spell.

## Strategy

**Eternal Night** is essentially a combat time-saver, and can also conserve some [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** after it's cast. Its entire point is that the caster will no longer need to cast [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Darkness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Darkness "Darkness")** each time a battle erupts in order to augment his/her units. Because **Eternal Night**
already puts this effect in place automatically at the start of each
and every battle, the player is free to cast other useful spells, and
more of them. It also applies in magical [Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Node "Node"), where casting [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Darkness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Darkness "Darkness")** otherwise may prove to be problematic.

As far as [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** expenditure concerns go, **Eternal Night** is fairly cost-efficient. Unless playing with the [Channeler](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Channeler "Channeler") [Retort](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort"), or relying heavily on [Caster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Caster "Caster") [Spellcaster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Caster "Caster") [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), fighting at least one battle each turn where [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Darkness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Darkness "Darkness")**
would be cast will generally make this spell worthwhile. The
time-saving quality explained above can further justify its costs.
Getting other spells out early in a battle may lead to much more
decisive victories.

Naturally, this spell is only effective if the player uses plenty of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**-aligned creatures and [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") units. Otherwise, there are no real benefits to casting it - except perhaps when fighting a war against a [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard"). Then again [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**-wielding players can rarely afford *not* to lead on plenty of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** creatures, so this point is rarely an issue. However, while battling other [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** Wizards, **Eternal Night** may become a double-edged sword.

Finally, note that once **Eternal Night** is cast, it should be prudent to cancel *all* existing [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Cloud of Shadow](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cloud_of_Shadow "Cloud of Shadow")** [Town Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town_Enchantment "Town Enchantment"). Both of these spells provide the same benefits and are not cumulative, meaning that **Eternal Night** turns [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Cloud of Shadow](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cloud_of_Shadow "Cloud of Shadow")** obsolete, which would then only serve to burn up an [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") each turn.
