**Unit Enchantment** is a [Spell Type](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Type "Spell Type") and category of spells in *Master of Magic*. Like all other enchantments, **Unit Enchantments**
are meant to have a persistent positive effect - lasting as long as the
enchantment remains in play. The spells in this group are intended to
be used on the caster's own units, either to improve their combat
performance, or to protect them from harm. As such, they must always be
targeted at a single friendly unit.

Most **Unit Enchantments**
have two "versions" - an overland, and a combat version. The overland
casting is more costly, but can continuously affect the unit for a long
period of time, albeit at a cost of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** that has to be paid every turn as [Upkeep](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost"). The combat casting on the other hand, while cheaper, bestows the spell's benefits only for the duration of a single battle.

There are a total of 33 different **Unit Enchantments** in *Master of Magic*. The [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm") specializes in these, having no less than 10 different spells of this type. This is followed closely by [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")** and [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**, with 8 each; while the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** and [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** Realms lean more towards destructive spells and [Unit Curses](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Curse "Unit Curse"), having only 4 and 3 **Unit Enchantments** respectively.

Rival wizards can attempt to strip all **Unit Enchantments**
from a target unit using dispel type magic, although these spells have
to succeed with separate dispelling attempts against each individual
enchantment in turn. **Unit Enchantments** may also be cancelled manually by their caster during their turn, while not in combat, to conserve [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** when necessary. If a unit is destroyed, in combat or otherwise, all **Unit Enchantments** affecting it will also dissipate instantly.

## Usage

With a few exceptions, **Unit Enchantments**
typically have two "versions". That is, they can be cast both on the
overland map, and in combat. Both versions have the same exact effect on
the target unit, but differ in their [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost"), [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") and the duration of the enchantment. They are thus used differently and for different purposes.

In either case however, the spell must be targeted at a single
friendly unit. Immediately after a successful casting, the target unit
receives all the benefits associated with the spell, and these will last
as long as the enchantment remains in place. A glowing outline also
surrounds the unit, with a color matching that of the spell's [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm"), although only one such aura can be visible at a time, corresponding to the most powerful enchantment affecting the unit.

### Overland Unit Enchantments

With the exception of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Berserk](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Berserk "Berserk")** and [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Haste](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Haste "Haste")**, all **Unit Enchantments**
can be cast on the overland map. If the desired target is grouped with
other units, clicking on the stack will bring up a list view of the
entire group, allowing the player to select the individual unit. The
game will not allow casting the same enchantment on a unit more than
once: attempting this will result in an immediate error message,
although a different target may still be chosen afterward.

The [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of **Unit Enchantments** is relatively low compared to other overland spells. However, to keep these spells in play, a Wizard must also pay an [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost"),
typically proportionate to the usefulness of the effect, at the
beginning of every overland turn. The game will automatically deduct
this from the player's [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana**
pool or income, but if it fails to do so, the enchantment will be
discarded, immediately removing all of its benefits from the unit.

Otherwise, **Overland Unit Enchantments** will last until cancelled, dispelled by an enemy, or until the enchanted unit is destroyed, in combat, or otherwise. **Unit Enchantments** can only be removed manually by their caster during their own turn, while not in combat. This requires opening the *unit statistics window* of the unit, and left-clicking on the spell's name or icon in the unit's list of abilities and effects.

While an **Overland Unit Enchantment** is affecting a unit,
its benefits will carry over from the campaign map into every battle the
unit participates in; and the spell will also continue to exist
afterward, as long as it isn't actively removed. Therefore, the unit
will start each battle already enjoying the effects of the enchantment,
and will continue to do so battle after battle.

This is the primary reason for casting a **Unit Enchantment** overland: it removes the need to spend additional time and [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana**
putting the same effects on the unit each time it goes into battle.
This is very useful when a unit is going to need those same bonuses to
survive, or to make it truly effective; but not when faced with a
single, atypical threat, that requires but a temporary adjustment to the
unit's abilities.

### Combat Unit Enchantments

The majority of **Unit Enchantments** can also be cast during a battle, and the two mentioned previously can *only*
be cast this way. While the beneficial effects of the enchantments are
exactly the same regardless of this circumstance, there are three main
casting attributes that are different. First of these is the spell's
duration: **Unit Enchantments** cast in combat are predetermined to always end when the battle does.

This means that a **Combat Unit Enchantment** will never carry over back onto the overland map. Consequently, there are no [Upkeep Costs](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost")
associated with enchantments cast this way, although as a side effect,
this also means that the caster is unable to cancel them manually, as
this action is not available during combat. However, the spell will
dissipate when the battle ends anyway, so this is rarely an issue.

To offset the preset duration, combat casting of **Unit Enchantments** is much cheaper. The [Casting Costs](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost") of the combat "versions" are always exactly 1/5th of the overland costs, and the spells are treated as having these *base* Casting Costs for all intents and purposes. This makes them possible to cast with a much lower [Spell Casting Skill](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Casting_Skill "Spell Casting Skill"), although in terms of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana**, this may not be the equivalent, unless the spell is cast by a unit. This is because [Wizards](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") are subject to *Casting Distance Multipliers*, which can affect the amount of [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** they need to channel while casting spells in combat.

Either way, **Unit Enchantments**
cast in combat must be targeted the same way as their overland
counterparts, and will always only affect a single friendly unit. Again,
units that already have the same enchantment are invalid targets,
regardless of whether the first spell was also cast in combat or on the
overland map. The enchantments are also subject to dispelling attempts
by enemies, and will dissipate automatically if the affected unit
perishes in the battle.

**Combat Unit Enchantments** are generally useful either for
providing impromptu benefits to a unit based on a specific situation
encountered in battle (e.g. [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[True Sight](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/True_Sight "True Sight")** when facing unexpected [Invisible](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility") units); or for utilizing units with the [link = Caster](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/2/2d/Ability_Caster.png/revision/latest?cb=20120123155035 "link = Caster") [Caster](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Caster "Caster") ability to provide the enchantments instead of the Wizard. Because of the *Casting Distance Multipliers* mentioned above, units fighting on distant lands (or on the other [Plane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Plane "Plane")) are often better off with an overland-cast **Unit Enchantment** if the same bonus is required in more than a single battle.

### The Enchantment Icon

Once a unit is affected by a **Unit Enchantment**, an icon representing this spell appears in the *unit statistics window* whenever the unit is inspected (by right-clicking on it). **Unit Enchantments**
can be easily distinguished from other icons by their purple
background, although there are also four such icons that do not, in
fact, represent a true enchantment (i.e. they can't be removed). On
occasion, a **Unit Enchantment** may add two icons instead of one, indicating a further set of abilities granted to the unit.

## Getting Rid of Unit Enchantments

There are 4 different ways for a **Unit Enchantment**
to dissipate, although one of these is slightly different depending on
whether the spell was cast overland or in combat. Regardless of how an
enchantment is removed though, any benefits that it granted will also be
immediately lost along with it.

### Cancelling Manually

As noted above, the owner of a **Unit Enchantment** can voluntarily cancel it, by opening the target's *unit statistics window*, finding the icon representing the **Unit Enchantment**,
and then left-clicking on it. However, this is only possible while on
the overland map, and thus can only remove the overland version of a **Unit Enchantment**. During combat, it is not possible to cancel any **Unit Enchantment** regardless of how it was cast.

### Duration Ends / Lack of Mana

**Unit Enchantments** cast on the overland map require an [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") to be paid in [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana**
at the start of each turn. If this is not possible, the spell will end,
much the same way that the combat-cast version dissipates automatically
at the end of the battle.

### Dispel Magic

Rival wizards can use any one of four different dispel type spells to try and remove **Unit Enchantments** from a unit. [Arcane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcane "Arcane")**[Dispel Magic](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Dispel_Magic "Dispel Magic")** and [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Dispel Magic True](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Dispel_Magic_True "Dispel Magic True")** are both single-target [Combat Instants](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Instant "Combat Instant"); while [Arcane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcane "Arcane")**[Disenchant Area](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Disenchant_Area "Disenchant Area")** and [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Disenchant True](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Disenchant_True "Disenchant True")**
can be cast either overland, to target all units on a map tile, or in
combat, to affect every unit on the battlefield (in addition to opposing
[Combat](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Enchantment "Combat Enchantment")-, and possibly [Town Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town_Enchantment "Town Enchantment")).

In either case, the game will make a separate [dispelling attempt](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost#Dispelling_Magic "Casting Cost") against each individual **Unit Enchantment**
on the target or, for the disenchants, every target. For each success,
that particular spell is removed, without affecting any of the ones for
which the attempt has failed. It may be worth noting that [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities"), such as [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity"), offer no protection against this. On the other hand, [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Spell Lock](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Lock "Spell Lock")** is designed for exactly this purpose, although the spell does unfortunately have some bugs in the latest official game version.

If a unit perishes, either in- or outside of combat, any and all **Unit Enchantments** it carries are immediately lost with it. One notable exception are [Regeneration](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration") [Regenerating](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Regeneration "Regeneration")

with their side suffering defeat. In this case, if they have any **Overland Unit Enchantments** cast on them, they *will* retain them if resurrected by Regeneration.

## Effects

**Unit Enchantments** can serve a multitude of functions, but will generally cause one or more of the following effects:

1. Increase the unit's combat attributes, such as [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength"), [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense**, or [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance**;
2. Duplicate one of the game's [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities"), such as [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") [Immolation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Immolation "Immolation") or [Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility") [Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility "Invisibility");
3. Protect the unit from [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**, or otherwise increase its survivability.

In all cases, a **Unit Enchantment** applies its benefits only to the specific targeted unit. For spells that affect more the one unit simultaneously, see [Combat Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Enchantment "Combat Enchantment") and [Global Enchantment](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Global_Enchantment "Global Enchantment").

### Unit Abilities

Several **Unit Enchantments** work by adding a new ability to the unit that it did not previously have. [Unit Abilities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Abilities "Unit Abilities") are listed in the bottom portion of the *unit statistics window*, and this is the same place where the **Unit Enchantment**'s own icon will appear. On occasion, the actual ability added by the **Unit Enchantment**
will also appear here. The color of the icon's background can typically
(but not always) be used to distinguish between innate (which the unit
possesses by default), and magical abilities (which it gained through **Unit Enchantments**): yellow indicates innate abilities, while purple indicates magical ones.

A complete list of the Unit Abilities available through a **Unit Enchantment**
can be found below. However, a unit may never have two instances of the
same ability. If it already possesses some ability, and then is
affected by a **Unit Enchantment**
that grants the same ability, then no new ability will be added.
Occasionally, the game will not allow such a unit to be targeted with
the enchantment, but this is usually not the case!

### Unique Effects

Some **Unit Enchantments**
provide an effect that is unique to that enchantment. That is, they
alter the basic game rules in one way or another, typically in a manner
that is not available in the game outside of these specific **Unit Enchantments**.

#### Berserk

:   [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Berserk](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Berserk "Berserk")** is one of the two combat-only **Unit Enchantments**. It doubles the target's [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee** [Attack Strength](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Attack_Strength "Attack Strength"), applied after all other calculations of the attribute; but at the same time, it also reduces the unit's [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **0**, again applied after all other modifiers. However, it's worth noting that conditional effects, including Immunities, *can* override the latter effect.

#### Eldritch Weapon

:   [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Eldritch Weapon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Eldritch_Weapon "Eldritch Weapon")** is the only effect in the game capable of *reducing* the base 30% chance [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **To Block**. Whenever a unit affected by this spell performs a [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Melee Attack** (including [Counter Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack")), [Thrown Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Thrown_Attack "Thrown Attack") **Thrown Attack**, or [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile Attack**, its target will suffer a temporary reduction of [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **-10%** for the duration of resolving the attack. In other words, each point of its [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **Defense** has only a base 20% chance of blocking a point of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage**.

#### Haste

:   In addition to doubling [Movement Allowance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Allowance "Movement Allowance") in combat, whenever a unit affected by this spell is ordered to attack, or is performing a [Counter Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Attack "Counter Attack"), it will make 2 attacks in rapid succession instead of the normal 1. However, [Ranged Magical Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Magical_Attack "Ranged Magical Attack") **Ranged Magical Attacks** are excluded from this, and other types of [Ranged Attacks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Attack "Ranged Attack") may still be subject to the limitations imposed by Ability Ammunition Ammunition (of which they will naturally use a double amount).

#### Heroism

:   This spell raises the [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Experience Level** of a [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") or [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") to [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **3** for as long as it is in effect, and will automatically dissipate once the unit naturally acquires enough [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") **Experience Points** to attain that level. It stacks with both [Warlord](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warlord "Warlord") and [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Crusade](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Crusade "Crusade")** to grant even higher levels, although this quirk will not work if [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Heroism](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Heroism "Heroism")** is cast in combat.

#### Invulnerability

:   In addition to granting [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity") [Weapon Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Weapon_Immunity "Weapon Immunity") as noted above, [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Invulnerability](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invulnerability "Invulnerability")** also automatically blocks [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **2** points of [Damage Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Damage_Points "Damage Points") **Damage** from any attack directed at the enchanted unit. That is, it acts as if the unit had an extra [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+2** with a [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **100%** block chance; roughly equivalent to [Defense](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense "Defense") **+7** with the base block chance of [Defense Roll](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") **30%**, except without the uncertainty of random rolls. This effect only applies when [Defense Rolls](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Defense_Roll "Defense Roll") can or could otherwise be made though.

#### Righteousness

:   This enchantment grants an effect that is nearly identical to that of [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity") [Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity "Magic Immunity"), except it only applies against spells and effects that are markedly associated with the [Realms](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm") of [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** or [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**.
    The game typically checks for the presence of this enchantment right
    after checking for the immunity, and branches to the same resolution if
    either is present.

#### Spell Lock

:   The main purpose of [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Spell Lock](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Lock "Spell Lock")** is to protect *other* enchantments cast on its target. In addition, if applied to a [Fantastic Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit"), it also provides immunity from the spells [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Banish](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Banish "Banish")**, [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Great Unsummoning](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Great_Unsummoning "Great Unsummoning")**, [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Dispel Evil](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Dispel_Evil "Dispel Evil")**, and [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Holy Word](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Holy_Word "Holy Word")**.

### Temporary and Permanent Effects

The majority of **Unit Enchantments** provide completely temporary effects. This means that whatever benefits are bestowed on a unit will only last as long as the **Unit Enchantment**
remains in play. If the spell is ever removed, its effects will
immediately disappear along with it. However, there are also four spells
in the game that can permanently alter a unit, although only one of
these is a true **Unit Enchantment**, and its permanent effect is almost completely separate from the real enchantment.

The first of these spells is [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Lycanthropy](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Lycanthropy "Lycanthropy")**, an overland [Instant Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Instant_Spell "Instant Spell") that is also commonly considered a [Summoning Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Summoning_Spell "Summoning Spell"). This transforms a [Normal Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") entirely into [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Werewolves](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Werewolves "Werewolves")**, leaving behind no trace of the original unit. As such, it has no listed effect in the *unit statistics window*, and can not be removed or undone.

The next two spells are closely related to each other, and are often considered to be forms of **Unit Enchantments**. These are [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")**, and its [Global](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Global_Enchantment "Global Enchantment") version [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Doom Mastery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Doom_Mastery "Doom Mastery")** which, while in effect, will automatically apply the first spell to *every* Normal Unit that its caster creates. [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos_Channels "Chaos Channels")** confers one of three *permanent* benefits to its target, but is *not* a true **Unit Enchantment**,
despite the fact that the icons of all of its effects have the purple
backgrounds that typically indicate these. Once cast, this spell can not
be removed short of destroying the unit, and has no [Upkeep Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Upkeep_Cost "Upkeep Cost") either; instead marking the unit as a creature of [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** with all the benefits and drawbacks that this entails (see the spell's description for more details).

Finally, [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Black Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Channels "Black Channels")** is the only true **Unit Enchantment** of this selection. However, this spell comes with a caveat: its bonuses will only affect the [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead") [Undead](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Undead "Undead").
This is neither an ability, nor a true enchantment (despite the icon's
purple background), but rather, an attribute that comes with multiple
strings attached, and is entirely permanent. When [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Black Channels](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Channels "Black Channels")** is cast on a unit without this trait (both [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") and [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero")
are valid targets for the spell), it will grant it. Permanently. Should
the enchantment itself be removed one way or another, the Undead
attribute will remain, and continue to exert all of its effects until
the unit is destroyed or the game ends.
