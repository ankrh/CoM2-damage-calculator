The [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**Life Realm** is one of the six magical [Realms](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm") in *Master of Magic*.
This realm focuses primarily on improving a wizard's assets - both
units and cities - making them stronger and/or repairing damage done to
them.

The **Life Realm** provides a wide array of [Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Type "Spell Type") for improving units and cities, but has far fewer [Instant Spells](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Instant_Spell "Instant Spell") and [Combat Instants](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Instant "Combat Instant"). Other than the [Arcane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcane "Arcane")**[Arcane Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcane "Arcane")**, it has the smallest variety of [Summoning Spells](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Summoning_Spell "Summoning Spell") in the game; Instead, the **Life Realm** is intended for use with large armies of [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit"), which must be augmented by this realm's spells and lead by the few powerful support units that **Life** does provide, making such armies far stronger than usual. The **Life Realm** is strongly opposed to the [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**, and may be either particularly effective against it or particularly vulnerable to it, depending on the circumstances.

The **Life Realm** is associated with the color white, and the concepts of devotion, strength, protection, and healing. It is marked with the [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**White Ankh** symbol, both in-game and on this wiki. The familiar associated with the **Life Realm** is the dove that appears at the top of this page.

## Overview

The [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**Life Realm** is the premier support color for [Cities](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town "Town"), [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit"), and [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"). There is much room for discussion over which **Race** serves a particular **Realm** best, but when one asks which **Realm** serves a **Race** best, the answer is always [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**Life**.

Life realm **Enchantments** inspire units with heightened
abilities and control travel between Arcanus and Myrror. They make
particularly deft use of the game's [Figure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Figure#Multi-Figure_Units "Figure") **Multi-Figure** and [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") **Experience**
mechanics. On the domestic side there are enchantments to raise
revenues, production, and growth, and eliminate unrest. There are some **Instant Spells** to heal the injured and revive the fallen.

Summoned creatures from this realm are not cost-effective at all
as primary troops; instead, individual creatures are meant to accompany
an army and augment its abilities through their presence. The ultimate
hero, [Torin](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Torin "Torin"), is available through this realm and can either continue in this role or serve as a *One-Man Army*.
In general, white is extremely strong in human hands thanks to its
ability to produce great quality in the absence of quantity.

**Life** also offers some direct counters to [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** and [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** magic. This color is so strongly antithetical to [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** in particular that wizards are barred from owning [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook") of both types.

### Description

*Master of Magic's* in-game documentation alludes in some
measure to the existence of a Life Realm, and there are grounds for
suspecting that good deities, or perhaps a monad of awesome strength,
exist in the backstory and are now starting to make moves in Arcanus and
Myrror. The planar landscape is probably suspended in astral space
above and between the two worlds, taking the form of a sovereign utopia—
civilized, erudite, and gorgeous.

While this plane cannot be entered and explored, it is the source of all [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**White Magic**
channeled by spellcasters, and the home of all Fantastic creatures of
life. These creatures answer to prayers and may form pacts of
allegiance with honorable spellcasters. If slain, discharged, or
otherwise unable to maintain a link to the earthly realms, they return
to the Life Realm revived to full power.

Magic can be channeled directly from this realm in the form of
light rays and divine energy. The Life Realm is tied as well to the
revelation of truth, and is likely the source of intelligent mortals'
"will to power." Aware of the potential in harnessing this school of
magic for glory and civilization, priests and magic-users from among the
advanced races study the Life Realm carefully.

## Realm Details

White has 10 spells per degree of [Spell Rarity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Rarity "Spell Rarity"), totaling 40 spells with a cumulative research cost of [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **59,950**. Each member holds a discrete cost increment along the spectrum between [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **20** ([Bless](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Bless "Bless")) and [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **6,000** ([Charm of Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Charm_of_Life "Charm of Life")).

White magic is heavy, almost to the point of exclusion, on instant and sustained spell effects targeting friendlies.

### Rival Wizards

:   *Main article: [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard")*

When the world is generated, computer-controlled wizards each make a lottery-ball style roll for [Personality](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Personality "Personality") and [Objective](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Objective "Objective").
These traits modify the wizard's behavior, giving them some
hard-to-resist compulsions and adding flavor to the match. If the
wizard's shelf is stocked primarily with [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**Life Spellbooks**,
this realm will convey influence on the rolls. In cases where
spellbook ranks are tied, and the game needs to decide which realm
conveys influence, precedence descends in this order:

:   [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature") [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery") [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos") [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life") [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")

Therefore, a default split-profile wizard like [Horus](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Horus "Horus") ([Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**5** [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**5**) will take a personality and objective corresponding to probabilities dictated by his sorcery ranks, not his life ones.

Dominant Life ranks drive the wizard towards a Personality of
"Peaceful" and an Objective of "Perfectionist" (but do not by any means
guarantee these traits). The table below shows the chances each
character trait has of appearing on a Life wizard, before accounting for
the influence of [Retorts](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort").

| [Icon Book Life](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/masterofmagic/images/a/a6/Icon_Book_Life.png/revision/latest?cb=20120102020209) | Personality | | Objective | |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Peaceful | 50% | Perfectionist | 40% |
| Lawful | 20% | Theurgist | 30% |
| Aggressive | 20% | Militarist | 20% |
| Ruthless | 10% | Expansionist | 10% |
| Chaotic | -- |
| Maniacal | -- |

Peaceful
:   On the diplomatic scale of 100 to -100, a "Peaceful" wizard has a +20 *Core Reaction*
    to all of the other wizards, including the human player, and the chance
    this wizard will elect to perform hostile actions (overland spells and
    armed assaults) is reduced by 20 percentage points. As all diplomatic
    standings gravitate towards the core reaction at a speed of 1 point per
    turn, it is therefore easier to maintain a quiet, if not friendly, front
    with this marshmallow of a wizard.

Perfectionist
:   A "Perfectionist" wizard has double the chance of initiating construction of a [Town Building](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town_Building "Town Building"),
    and a correspondingly reduced chance of commencing work on a new unit.
    The wizard is also dedicated to throwing positive enchantments on his
    cities, when these are available.

White wizards can usually be left alone in favor of more threatening
opponents. While they will be very strong if allowed to prosper
quietly, this realm does not give them any tools for nuking their
enemies with crippling magic.

### Fantastic Units

The [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**Life Realm** has only four native [Fantastic Creature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") types, and a corresponding overland [Summoning Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Summoning_Spell "Summoning Spell")
for each degree of rarity. These units have increasingly-huge support
capabilities and maintenance costs. They are a bit expensive to cast
and maintain *en masse*. One [Hero](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") is also considered a fantastic unit of the Life Realm for most purposes, and is available through his own [Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rare_Spell "Rare Spell") summoning spell.

The [Guardian Spirit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Guardian_Spirit "Guardian Spirit") plays a more extensive role than the higher units in this realm. While a [Magic Spirit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Spirit "Magic Spirit") may be cheaper, the Guardian is ideal for melding any [Node](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Node "Node")
that might come to be within a rival's reach. It can be taken as a
starting spell in a variety of wizard builds, and makes a formidable
combatant in the early stages of the game.

The [Unicorns](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unicorns "Unicorns"), [Angel](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Angel "Angel"), and [Arch Angel](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arch_Angel "Arch Angel") are support-and-strike units, but the last two may be preempted by [Incarnation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Incarnation "Incarnation"),
which is not only cheaper to research and cast, but also creates the
mightiest unit in the entire game. ­Still, nothing is keeping you from
backing up [Torin](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Torin "Torin") with an Arch Angel.

Every lair makes a procedural selection of guardians through what is essentially a [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** budget, when it can afford guardians. Typically there will be one or more high-end units of the lair's [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm") (this is the creature sighted by scouts who peek into the lair), attended by a lesser unit type. The [Ancient Temple](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ancient_Temple "Ancient Temple"), [Fallen Temple](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fallen_Temple "Fallen Temple"), and [Ruins](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ruins "Ruins") will harbor [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** creatures most of the time. The [Tower](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Tower "Tower")
can have anything; the world generator always places six towers, so
about two-thirds of the time, a map is going to have at least one tower
populated with creatures of [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**Life**.

Note that a white encounter zone never ejects [Rampaging Monsters](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rampaging_Monster "Rampaging Monster").
The idea of a horde of angels and unicorns attacking a village and
cutting down every last soul in the place is, perhaps, an awkward
interpretation of this realm's ideology. *Any* other unconquered lair on the map is a candidate for hosting this periodic event.

    We have to suppose that these mechanics are unintentional, because
    life ranks are needed for such an inordinate number of high-quality
    pieces of gear.

### Related Wizard Skills

#### [Divine Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Divine_Power "Divine Power")

**Divine Power** requires [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**4** ranks in Life magic and costs 2 picks. It increases the power and the pacifying effects obtained from [Shrines](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Shrine "Shrine"), [Temples](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Temple "Temple"), [Parthenons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Parthenon "Parthenon"), and [Cathedrals](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cathedral "Cathedral") by 50%. While it is redundant with [Stream of Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stream_of_Life "Stream of Life"), and lacks the synergy of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Dark Rituals](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Dark_Rituals "Dark Rituals")** with [Infernal Power](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Infernal_Power "Infernal Power"), an economy driven by Life magic enables these expensive buildings to be constructed in a hurry.

**Infernal Power** may be found by Life wizards and yields exactly the same effect as **Divine Power**. The two retorts together do not exhibit cumulative effects.

#### [Warlord](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Warlord "Warlord")

**Warlord** has no prerequisites and costs 2 picks. It lowers [Experience](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience "Experience") **Exp** requirements, effectively raising the level of all units under the wizard's control by one, and unlocks higher levels for **Normal Units**. This is the ideal realm for this retort, most notably on account of the [Heroism](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Heroism "Heroism") and [Crusade](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Crusade "Crusade") spells. Through a **Warlord**, the former can be used to promote units to [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Ultra-Elite** in the very beginning of the game, while the latter unlocks the [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Champion** level when cast. The retort also has synergy with [Altar of Battle](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Altar_of_Battle "Altar of Battle") and the general emphasis Life places on **Normal Units** and the **Cities** producing and backing them up.

#### [Alchemy](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Alchemy_(Retort) "Alchemy (Retort)")

**Alchemy** has no prerequisites and costs 1 pick. It allows [Gold](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gold "Gold") **Gold** and [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** to be converted at a one-to-one ratio, and equips all units built in cities with [Melee Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Melee_Attack "Melee Attack") **Magical Weapons**.
Both benefits should appeal to Life wizards; the first because they
can roll their strong tax revenues over into mana, and the second
because they are likely to be building **Normal Units** early on.
