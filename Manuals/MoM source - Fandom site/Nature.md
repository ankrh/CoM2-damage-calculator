The [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**Nature Realm** is one of the six magical [Realms](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm") in *Master of Magic*. **Nature**
is somewhat of a jack-of-all-trades school, though its forte may be in
summoning whole armies of powerful magical creatures to stampede
opponents. The Nature Realm has numerous [Summoning Spells](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Summoning_Spell "Summoning Spell") at all [Rarity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Rarity "Spell Rarity") levels, and the creatures accessed by casting them are mobile and tough.

The other spells of this school are directed at the ground, as
often as not, and offer more esoteric effects and tools to the wizard.
These include minor protections, snares in combat, fast exploration, and
even wholesale terraforming. Nature is mildly opposed to Chaos; while
wizards are not barred from owning spellbooks of both colors, green
offers some direct counters to red.

A very practical color for the human player, this realm buffs his followers (though not as over-the-top as [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**), hits hard (though not with the force of [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** or [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**), and plays unconventionally (though not so much so as [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**). Experienced players who choose to forego nature books altogether tend to miss them.

### Description

*Master of Magic's* documentation declares that green magic is
"rooted in the land," a part of Arcanus and Myrror. There may still be a
Nature Realm, a lush parallel plane of existence that embodies pure
natural concepts, powers the Nature Nodes, and serves as home to the
various Fantastic Creatures linked with this school. These beings are
decidedly supernatural, with qualities like impossible size and strength
and the ability to turn enemies to stone. They are sustained in the
mundane world with binding rituals and a steady infusion of mana.

The Nature Realm is the object of both study and worship by
animistic cults in the game, and by any agency opposed to Chaos.
Recognizing the value of good crop yields at home and a resilient front
line, even wizards with no interest in this school of magic may bankroll
druidic and shamanic orders in their territories. Nature magic seems
to manifest in the evocation of biomatter, water, earth, and ice, and
lends itself well to peaceful endeavors. It offers its share of violent
ways to maintain the status-quo, as well.

Familiar
:   A wizard with mainly Nature spellbooks will take a Serpent as a
    familiar. Serpents are an ancient symbol of death and creation in
    cultures across the world. The green hue signifies fertility.

## Realm Details

Nature has 10 spells per degree of [Spell Rarity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Rarity "Spell Rarity"), totaling 40 spells with a cumulative research cost of [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **59,950**. Each member holds a discrete cost increment along the spectrum between [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **20** ([Earth to Mud](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Earth_to_Mud "Earth to Mud")) and [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **6,000** ([Nature's Wrath](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature%27s_Wrath "Nature's Wrath")).

Green spells have a tendency to aim directly at the map tiles,
for the benefit of the caster's side or the frustration of his enemies.
It also has a variety of direct summoning spells.

### Rival Wizards

:   *Main article: [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard")*

When the world is generated, computer-controlled wizards each make a lottery-ball style roll for [Personality](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Personality "Personality") and [Objective](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Objective "Objective").
These traits modify the wizard's behavior, giving them some
hard-to-resist compulsions and adding flavor. If the wizard's shelf is
stocked primarily with [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**Nature Spellbooks**,
this realm will influence the rolls, tending the wizard towards a
Personality of "Lawful" and an Objective of "Perfectionist" (but no
guarantees). In cases where spellbook ranks are tied, and the game
needs to decide which realm conveys influence, precedence descends in
this order:

:   [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature") [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery") [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos") [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life") [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")

Since Nature takes precedence over the other colors, a default split-profile wizard like [Merlin](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Merlin "Merlin") ([Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**5** [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**5**) will take a personality and objective corresponding to probabilities dictated by his Nature ranks, not his Life ones.

The table below shows the chances each character trait has of
appearing on a Nature wizard, before accounting for the influence of [Retorts](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort").

| [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature") | Personality | | Objective | |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Lawful | 50% | Perfectionist | 40% |
| Peaceful | 20% | Theurgist | 20% |
| Aggressive | 10% | Militarist | 20% |
| Ruthless | 10% | Expansionist | 20% |
| Chaotic | 10% |
| Maniacal | -- |

Lawful
:   On the diplomatic scale of 100 to -100, a "Lawful" wizard has a +10 *Core Reaction*
    to all of the other wizards, including the human player. Before
    performing a hostile act, this wizard formally nullifies treaties. The
    chance of this happening is reduced by 40 percentage points (usually to
    zero or less) unless the player has violated a treaty before, in which
    case it is *increased* by 40 percentage points. Diplomatic point
    losses are always doubled with a "Lawful" wizard. Generally, these
    wizards are a ticking time bomb... softer, even, than "Peaceful" until
    relations dip negative or a treaty gets broken, at which point they get
    extremely belligerent.

Perfectionist
:   A "Perfectionist" wizard has double the chance of initiating construction of a [Town Building](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Town_Building "Town Building"),
    and a correspondingly reduced chance of commencing work on a new unit.
    The wizard is also dedicated to throwing positive enchantments on his
    cities, when these are available.

Aggression against computer-controlled green wizards may need special
preparation. Nature spells are surprisingly vicious in combat, and on
high difficulty settings, these players build up a strong
military-industrial concern. Since the wizards are benign and do not
gain any overland attacks through this realm until late, you have time
to prepare.

#### Green Diplomacy Modifiers

Rival wizards make one initial adjustment to their *Core Reaction* to a green wizard (human or otherwise):

|  |
| --- |
| * **+2** per [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**Nature Spellbook** shared in common with the rival |

*Example:* [Tlaloc](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Tlaloc "Tlaloc") adjusts his *Core Reaction* to [Freya](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Freya "Freya") by +8 ([Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**4** held in common)
Leader personalities, retorts, and other spellbooks will make further adjustments.

### Fantastic Units

There are eleven [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") associated with [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**Nature**, all of which are vanguard attack units designed for muscle in combat. One, the [Earth Elemental](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Earth_Elemental "Earth Elemental"), is a combat-only summon, while the rest are called into the game by ordinary overland [Summoning Spells](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Summoning_Spell "Summoning Spell"). The Earth Elemental is the most hard-hitting and durable combat summon there is, but it's a [Rare Spell](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rare_Spell "Rare Spell").

The Common entries, [War Bears](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/War_Bears "War Bears") and [Sprites](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sprites "Sprites"), can fill substantial roles when selected as starting spells. The **War Bears** are a strike unit, on par perhaps with a generic [Experience Level](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Level "Experience Level") **Elite** [Cavalry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cavalry "Cavalry") unit, that may come into sudden demand with the appearance of a [Mountaineer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mountaineer "Mountaineer") (since the bears are [Foresters](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Forester "Forester"), the combined stack achieves [Pathfinding](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Pathfinding "Pathfinding")). The **Sprites**
are a higher-budget item, able to fly and pelt enemies with four
magical volleys per battle; they can explore for choice targets of
conquest early on, and may even be able to lay siege to places without
being at risk themselves.

The Uncommon [Giant Spiders](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Giant_Spiders "Giant Spiders"), [Cockatrices](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Cockatrices "Cockatrices"), and [Basilisk](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Basilisk "Basilisk") are expensive to research and produce. They test enemy [Resistance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resistance "Resistance") **Resistance** and make good company for heroes and mixed attack forces. The Rare [Stone Giant](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Stone_Giant "Stone Giant") and [Gorgons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gorgons "Gorgons") play to the same tune. The three Very Rare members of this realm, the [Behemoth](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Behemoth "Behemoth"), [Colossus](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Colossus "Colossus"), and [Great Wyrm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Great_Wyrm "Great Wyrm"), are gigantic monsters, with high attack, defense, and hitpoint scores.

Most Nature Realm creatures have high initiative. To begin with, they have a [Movement Allowance](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Movement_Allowance "Movement Allowance") of at least [Ground Movement](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ground_Movement "Ground Movement")**2** (or in the case of the **Earth Elemental**, he can appear on a chosen combat tile and attack immediately). The **Great Wyrm** can also teleport anywhere on the battlefield. The **Giant Spiders** can snare enemy units, and the **Basilisk** can attack flying units because it has a [Gaze Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Gaze_Attack "Gaze Attack").
Some of the units have strong ranged attacks or stoning attacks
designed to bully the enemy before melee begins, and the units of the
realm are generally quite good in melee.

### Nature Encounter Zones

Every lair makes a procedural selection of guardians through what is essentially a [Mana](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mana "Mana") **Mana** budget. When it can afford to stock defenders at all, there will typically be one or more higher-end units of the lair's [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm") (this is the creature sighted by scouts who peek into the lair), attended by a lesser unit type. [Abandoned Keeps](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Abandoned_Keep "Abandoned Keep"), [Dungeons](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Dungeon "Dungeon"), [Monster Lairs](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Monster_Lair "Monster Lair"), and [Mysterious Caves](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mysterious_Cave "Mysterious Cave") always roll Nature creatures. [Towers of Wizardry](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Tower_of_Wizardry "Tower of Wizardry")
can have anything; the world generator always places six towers, so
about two-thirds of the time, a map is going to have at least one green
magic tower.

[Nature Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature_Node "Nature Node")
always have Nature Realm creatures. In total, 16 Nodes appear on
Arcanus and 14 on Myrror, and any one of these could be a Nature Node
with a substantial garrison.

Nature has a fairly high minimum cost for generating a creature in a lair (**War Bears** at 70 points). Some lairs will therefore be undefended.

Any unconquered lair with Nature Realm creatures can eject a horde of [Rampaging Monsters](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rampaging_Monster "Rampaging Monster").
This will be a randomly-generated Nature group whose strength will
correspond with the number of game turns elapsed and has nothing to do
with the lair's defenders. The turn after it appears, it will make for a
nearby city and attack. The group can consist of any number of
different species of Nature units.
