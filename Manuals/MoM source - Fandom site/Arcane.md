The [Arcane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcane "Arcane")**Arcane Realm** is one of the six magical [Realms](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm") in *Master of Magic*.
It is much more of a "utility" school of magic, which can complement
any other magical realm with the use of several spells that are not
associated with any particular concept.

Each [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") begins the game with two **Arcane** spells already available, and any Wizard may research all **Arcane** spells by the end of the game. The **Arcane Realm**
contains only a handful of spells, but many of these are necessary to
most Wizards in order to perform more "mundane" activities that other
schools of magic do not - such as summoning [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), creating [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item"), and manipulating the [Summoning Circle](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Summoning_Circle "Summoning Circle").

Furthermore, a Wizard can win the game by researching and casting the [Spell of Mastery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_of_Mastery "Spell of Mastery") - a [Very Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare "Very Rare") **Arcane** spell with exceptionally high [Research-](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") and [Casting Cost](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Casting_Cost "Casting Cost").

The **Arcane Realm** is associated with the color grey (or
tan), and is not aligned with any specific elements or concepts. It is
often marked with the [Arcane](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Arcane "Arcane")**Tan Circumpunct** symbol, both in-game and on this Wiki. The **Arcane Realm** has no familiar, nor does it have any [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook"); a Wizard cannot be "associated" with the **Arcane Realm**, though he/she may opt to possess and use only **Arcane** spells, relying on [Retorts](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Retort "Retort"), [Normal Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Normal_Unit "Normal Unit") and [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") to win.

## Overview

This realm consists of 14 special utility spells. Wizards begin the game with two of them, [Magic Spirit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Spirit "Magic Spirit") and the [Spell of Return](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_of_Return "Spell of Return"), and have full access to the rest of this color through research. There are no arcane [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook").

Grey magic is the key to end *Master of Magic* in a glorious "space-race" victory, by way of the [Spell of Mastery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_of_Mastery "Spell of Mastery").

Leaning heavily on grey spells is nothing to be ashamed of,
because this realm offers very useful toys. It is needed, in varying
degrees depending on your build, to explore around and eventually to
meld [Nodes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Node "Node") with the **Magic Spirit**, summon and recall [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero"), detect enemy spellcasting, fizzle enemy enchantments, move the [Summoning Circle](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Summoning_Circle "Summoning Circle") around, craft [Magical Items](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magical_Item "Magical Item"), and in an emergency, to reestablish your lost [Fortress](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fortress "Fortress").

The human player should make a point of brokering grey spells around with the AI rivals, for several good reasons:

1. They can get them anyway...
2. They might be willing to trade realm spells we cannot research, and...
3. We can put these grey spells to more creative uses than they typically do.

### Description

Those spells which do not draw up power from a specific magical realm
are deemed "Arcane." The full incantations are probably not scribbled
in the margins of the colored spellbooks on the wizard's shelf. Arcane
magic is more likely a natural offshoot of his training, entirely
improvised. It opens the wizard's game with a singularly important
event: the establishment of his Enchanted Fortress and its summoning
circle.

The stronger arcane spells demand increasingly obscure techniques
to develop and cast. The humble Dispel Magic merely draws upon a
wizard's know-how in sustaining enchantments to sever those of his
rivals. Whereas, the ability to Create Artifacts will test the limits
of his specialized knowledge, technical ingenuity, and his ability to
channel vast amounts of mana with finesse.

Finally, Arcane effects seem peculiarly tied to the wizard's
level of insight into the mystical framework holding the game's universe
together, with such disparate repercussions as Detect Magic, Awareness,
Magic Spirit melding, and Summon Champion, as well as the Runemaster
skill. For its part, the Spell of Mastery combines this with lore
gained through research; the wider a wizard's spell knowledge, the
easier it is to develop this final weapon and take full charge of the
universe.

## Realm Details

This realm contains 2 *Known* spells and 12 *Learnable* ones, with a total research cost of [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **5,950** excluding the **Spell of Mastery**.

The cost of the game's final spell is [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **60,000**
minus half of the research values of all spells gathered, by any means,
during the campaign (starting spells not included). Starting with 11
books also deducts [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **3,000**.

Some grey magic effects can be approximated or surpassed by the spells in more exclusive realms. These are:

* [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Guardian Spirit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Guardian_Spirit "Guardian Spirit")** - a beefed-up **Magic Spirit**
* [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Incarnation](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Incarnation "Incarnation")** - summons a single-instance **Champion**
* [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life Force](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life_Force "Life Force")**, [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Tranquility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Tranquility "Tranquility")** - global oppressive effects as **Disjunction** vs. spells
* [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Word of Recall](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Word_of_Recall "Word of Recall")** - generalized **Recall Hero**, for all units and possible overland usage
* [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Dispel Magic True](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Dispel_Magic_True "Dispel Magic True")** - a triple-strength **Dispel Magic**
* [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Disenchant True](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Disenchant_True "Disenchant True")** - a triple-strength **Disenchant Area**
* [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Disjunction True](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Disjunction_True "Disjunction True")** - a triple-strength **Disjunction**
* [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Time Stop](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Time_Stop "Time Stop")**, [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Suppress Magic](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Suppress_Magic "Suppress Magic")** - global oppressive magic, as a miniaturized **Spell of Mastery**
* [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Move Fortress](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Move_Fortress "Move Fortress")** - relocates the Fortress in a less embarrassing manner than the **Spell of Return**
* [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature Awareness](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature_Awareness "Nature Awareness")** - total map **Awareness**
