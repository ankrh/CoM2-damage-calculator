The **Sorcery Realm** is one of the six Magical [Realms](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm") in *Master of Magic*. **Sorcery**
is themed around creating illusions and manipulating ephemeral features
of the environment, such as air, magicka, and the passage of time.
Meant for waging a cold war against other Wizards and their forces, **Sorcery** spells focus on opportunistic shutdown and defense.

The mark of the Realm is a [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**Blue Sparkle** that appears on the bindings of **Sorcery** [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook") in-game, and denotes **Sorcery** spells, effects, and "Spellbook Ranks" on this wiki.

### Description

Sorcery appears to be a realm of elemental magic, like Nature and
Chaos, that is connected in some way to the natural history of Arcanus
and Myrror. There may be a "Sorcery Realm," a cosmic source of power for
the Sorcery Nodes dotting the landscape, but the in-game help is more
explicit about the existence of a "Realm of Air": the home plane of
various aerial creatures which a practitioner may bind into service; and
the basis of many spell effects from this school.

Like the Arcane, Sorcery also governs some unknown, ethereal
dimension or context, from which sorcerers channel power to produce
exotic effects, such as illusions and warps in space, time, and
perceptions. The manual's so-called "meta-magic" arts feature
prominently in Sorcery as well: a line of research into sustained
magical barriers and counterspells, bigger and better than plain old
Arcane Dispel effects.

Sorcery does not seem to inspire the game's races towards an
ideology around the magic - very much unlike the realms of Life, Death,
Chaos, and Nature. A truly academic realm, it only attracts a few wizard
and hero-practitioners, perhaps being accounted too strange a
discipline for ordinary mortals to apply in civil and military work.

## Acquisition

The player may pick up to [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**11** **Sorcery** ranks when creating a new game. Additional [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook") may appear from time to time in [Treasure](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Treasure "Treasure") from [Encounter Zones](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Encounter_Zone "Encounter Zone"). **Sorcery** is unique among the five "colored" [Realms](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm") in that it is not directly opposed to any of the others.

Selecting just one **Sorcery** book or two will unlock random **Sorcery** spells for research and fulfill requirements for certain [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") abilities, while leaving room to specialize in something else. For each Spellbook rank taken above the first, one [Common](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Common_Spell "Common Spell") **Sorcery** spell may be selected to be *Known* from the start. The most general-purpose spells on the Common tier, such as [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Word of Recall](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Word_of_Recall "Word of Recall")**, [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Phantom Warriors](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Phantom_Warriors "Phantom Warriors")**, and [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Confusion](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Confusion "Confusion")**, are all popular initial picks.

Picking more books allows more spells to be *Known* at the beginning, eventual access to the Realm's [rarest](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Rarity "Spell Rarity") spells, and improved research and casting ability in **Sorcery**. In addition to widening spell knowledge, the middle ranks unlock strong enchantments for use in [Item Crafting](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Crafting "Item Crafting"). **Sorcery** is a favorable school of focus for boosting [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero") and "homemade" equipment in this manner.

**Sorcery** is rather
slow until the Wizard has a large selection of spells for use in various
circumstances, but it possesses some of the most powerful spells in the
game at the higher levels. It has a few, very pointed, [Summoning Spells](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Summoning_Spell "Summoning Spell") and [Unit Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment"), and instead focuses on presenting a wide variety of [Instant Spells](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Instant_Spell "Instant Spell") and [Combat Instants](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Combat_Instant "Combat Instant") - but again has some of the best [Very Rare Spells](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare_Spell "Very Rare Spell") in each category.

## Realm Details

:   *Main article: [List of Spells](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/List_of_Spells "List of Spells")*

**Sorcery** has 10 spells per degree of [Spell Rarity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Rarity "Spell Rarity"), totaling 40 spells with a cumulative research cost of [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **59,950**. Each member holds a discrete cost increment along the spectrum between [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **20** ([Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Resist Magic](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resist_Magic "Resist Magic")**) and [Research Points](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Research_Points "Research Points") **6,000** ([Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Time Stop](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Time_Stop "Time Stop")**).

### General Pros and Cons

**Sorcery** is a strategic toolbox. Most of the strongest [Wizard](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") builds — such as they are regarded by the *Master of Magic* player base — will either start the game with, or leave room for, some **Sorcery** [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook"), to get access to favored spells and effects.

Pros:

* Great for mobility ([Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Enchant Road](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Enchant_Road "Enchant Road")**, [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Flight](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Flight "Flight")**, [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Word of Recall](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Word_of_Recall "Word of Recall")**, [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Floating Island](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Floating_Island "Floating Island")**, [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Wind Walking](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wind_Walking_(Spell) "Wind Walking (Spell)")**);
* Superb for control (dispelling enemy spells / preventing enemy spells / preventing own spells from being dispelled);
* Grants unique unit abilities ([Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Guardian Wind](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Guardian_Wind "Guardian Wind")**, [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility_(Spell) "Invisibility (Spell)")**, [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity_(Spell) "Magic Immunity (Spell)")**, [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Haste](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Haste "Haste")**) that can be used on items, too;
* Versatile effects - all [Common Spells](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Common_Spell "Common Spell") have some usage, with an expanding repertoire covering more and more situations;
* Good combat summons ([Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Phantom Warriors](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Phantom_Warriors "Phantom Warriors")**, [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Phantom Beasts](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Phantom_Beast "Phantom Beast")**, [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Air Elementals](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Elemental "Air Elemental")**);
* The [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sky Drake](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sky_Drake "Sky Drake")**.

Cons:

* Terrible against [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** creatures ([Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity") [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity") negates [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Confusion](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Confusion "Confusion")**, [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Mind Storm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Mind_Storm "Mind Storm")**, and [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility_(Spell) "Invisibility (Spell)")**; and prevents armor penetration by [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Phantom Warriors](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Phantom_Warriors "Phantom Warriors")** and [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Psionic Blast](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Psionic_Blast "Psionic Blast")**) — [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")** creatures are seen in [Encounter Zones](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Encounter_Zone "Encounter Zone") very frequently;
* No spells for improving the economy;
* No spells for improving unit stats;
* Paucity of overland [Summoning Spells](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Summoning_Spell "Summoning Spell"), which can backfire with a low number of books;
* [Fantastic Units](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") are "glass cannons," hitting hard, but low on hitpoint scores themselves;
* The [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sky Drake](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sky_Drake "Sky Drake")**, again - usually have to defeat these to find extra **Sorcery** [Spellbooks](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook").

### Unit Enchantments

The **Sorcery** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm")'s positive [Unit Enchantments](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Enchantment "Unit Enchantment") bestow unusual defenses to friendly target units. The [Common](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Common_Spell "Common Spell") members of the group, [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Resist Magic](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Resist_Magic "Resist Magic")** and [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Guardian Wind](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Guardian_Wind "Guardian Wind")**, are just for contingencies: they might save a unit against [Unit Curses](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_Curse "Unit Curse") or standard [Ranged Missile Attack](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Ranged_Missile_Attack "Ranged Missile Attack") **Ranged Missile** attacks (respectively), or the occasion simply might not arise.

Further along though, [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Flight](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Flight "Flight")**, [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Invisibility](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Invisibility_(Spell) "Invisibility (Spell)")**, [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Magic Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Immunity_(Spell) "Magic Immunity (Spell)")**, [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Wind Walking](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wind_Walking_(Spell) "Wind Walking (Spell)")**, and [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Haste](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Haste "Haste")** are essential research goals - expensive, game-changing spells meant for valuable units. **Wind Walking** and **Flight** greatly improve overland movement for armies and increase liberties in battle. **Magic Immunity** and **Invisibility** shield the unit from many kinds of threats.

An extra layer of defense, the [Uncommon](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Uncommon_Spell "Uncommon Spell") [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Spell Lock](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Lock "Spell Lock")** can help these spells resist the Dispelling attempts of rival Wizards and [Heroes](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Hero "Hero").

### Fantastic Units

The **Sorcery** [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm") has eight native [Fantastic Unit](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Fantastic_Unit "Fantastic Unit") types. Its Summoning Spell repertoire emphasizes [Combat Summoning](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Summoning_Spell#Combat_Summoning_Spells "Summoning Spell").

The combat summons are [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Phantom Warriors](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Phantom_Warriors "Phantom Warriors")**, [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Phantom Beast](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Phantom_Beast "Phantom Beast")**, and [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Air Elemental](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Air_Elemental "Air Elemental")**; respectively Common, Uncommon, and [Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Rare_Spell "Rare Spell")
entries. They deal damage and draw the enemy's attention away from
other units on the field. They can provide pinch-defense in a remote or
poorly-defended [City](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/City "City"), lessening the need for big garrisons across the map.

The [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Nagas](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nagas "Nagas")**, [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Storm Giant](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Storm_Giant "Storm Giant")**, and [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Djinn](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Djinn "Djinn")**, respectively Common, Rare, and [Very Rare](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Very_Rare_Spell "Very Rare Spell"), are sturdier and more expensive creatures available through overland summoning. More expensive still is the [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Sky Drake](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sky_Drake "Sky Drake")**, one of the three elemental dragons at the top of the Fantastic Unit hierarchy.

**Sorcery**'s oddball summon is the Common [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Floating Island](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Floating_Island "Floating Island")** spell. It's essentially a huge [Transport](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Transport "Transport") that can be conjured overland on any visible water tile.

Usually there is no need to wipe out a computer player solely on the basis of them wielding **Sorcery**. Any rivals with shared spellbooks can also become valuable spell trading partners. If someone begins casting [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Spell Blast](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spell_Blast "Spell Blast")**, [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Suppress Magic](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Suppress_Magic "Suppress Magic")** or [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Time Stop](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Time_Stop "Time Stop")**, however, they probably need to be banished right away in an all-out assault.

Wizards with lots of **Sorcery** spells at their disposal can cause expensive problems on the battle screen. They prioritize [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**[Counter Magic](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Counter_Magic "Counter Magic")**
and "True" disenchantment; they may be able to attack with illusionary,
invisible, or otherwise tweaked units; or find some other way to be
annoying.

All computer players spend as much spell skill as they can, in every battle. The best way to deal with a **Sorcery** Wizard is either to bring overwhelming secular force, employ units with [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity") [Illusions Immunity](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Illusions_Immunity "Illusions Immunity"), or force them to fight a lot of pointless battles.

### Traits

When the world is generated, the computer-controlled [Wizards](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Wizard "Wizard") each make a lottery-ball style roll for **Personality** and **Objective**.
These traits modify the Wizard's behavior, giving them some
hard-to-resist compulsions and adding flavor. If the Wizard's shelf is
stocked primarily with **Sorcery** books, then this [Realm](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Realm "Realm") will determine how these lottery-ball rolls are weighted. **Sorcery** tends the Wizard towards a **Personality** of "Aggressive" and an **Objective** of "Theurgist" (but no guarantees). In cases where [Spellbook](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Spellbook "Spellbook") ranks are tied, and the game needs to decide which Realm conveys influence, precedence descends in this order:

:   [Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")**[Nature](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Nature "Nature")** [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")****Sorcery**** [Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")**[Chaos](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos")** [Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")**[Life](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Life "Life")** [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**

For instance, [Kali](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Kali "Kali")'s default [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**5** [Sorcery](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcery "Sorcery")**5** [Artificer](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Artificer "Artificer") build is a tie between two colors, but the game will weight her personality and objective according to her **Sorcery** ranks, not [Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**[Death](https://masterofmagic.fandom.com/wiki/Death "Death")**.
